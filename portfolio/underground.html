Introduction

In January 2016, there was no Wifi connectivity in the Downtown Seattle Transit Tunnel (DSTT). The problem we are trying to solve is finding a way to let people know when their transportation is arriving without being connected via internet or cell tower. The goal is to create a dashboard that inform commuters of when their bus or Link Light Rail is arriving and/or any transit delays. This dashboard will be frequently updated and will be displayed in multiple locations in the tunnel.

Field Research

At the University Street Station, we conducted observations and interviews with passengers on what they would need on a dashboard:

  1. Directionality of tunnel station
  2. Name of tunnel station, current time, date, and weather
  3. Number of stops away
  4. Warning symbol for bus delays
  5. Expected arrival time
  6. Name of bus route

User Research

We tested a paper prototype of our dashboard at the University Street station. Some of the comments given by commuters are listed below:

  • "This is great! I would love to know when a bus is coming"
  • "I like the large letters and colors, the current signs are hard to see!"
  • "The color and symbols are confusing. A legend would be helpful."
  • "The headphone jack is really accessble and ADA accessble."
